What Are Integers?

Integers are whole numbers that can be positive, negative, or zero. They do NOT include fractions or decimals.

Integers: {..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...} Positive integers: 1, 2, 3, 4, ... Negative integers: -1, -2, -3, -4, ... Zero: 0 (neither positive nor negative)

Number Line & Absolute Value

The number line helps visualize integers. Numbers to the right are greater; numbers to the left are smaller.

Absolute value is the distance from zero (always positive):

|5| = 5 |-5| = 5 |0| = 0

What is a Variable?

A variable is a letter that represents an unknown number. Common variables: x, y, n, a, b

Variable: a letter representing a number Constant: a fixed number (3, -5, 12) Coefficient: number multiplying a variable (in 5x, 5 is the coefficient) Term: a number, variable, or product (3, x, 7y) Expression: combination of terms (3x + 7)

Writing Expressions

Translate words into algebraic expressions:

Multiplication:

  • "4 times x" ‚Üí 4x
  • "product of 6 and y" ‚Üí 6y

Division:

What is an Equation?

An equation is a mathematical statement that two expressions are equal, connected by an equals sign (=).

Equation: x + 5 = 12 Solution: the value that makes the equation true (x = 7) Goal: isolate the variable on one side
üí° Balance Rule: Whatever you do to one side, you MUST do to the other side!

One-Step Equations

1

Move all variable terms to one side, constants to the other.

Example ‚Äî Solve: 5x - 3 = 2x + 12

What is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities. It can be written three ways:

3 to 4 or 3:4 or 3/4

Example: If there are 12 boys and 15 girls, the ratio of boys to girls is 12:15, which simplifies to 4:5.

A proportion states that two ratios are equal.

Example ‚Äî Solve for x: 3/5 = x/20

1

Unit Rates

A unit rate is a ratio where the second term is 1.

Example ‚Äî Find unit rate: $24 for 3 hours

Coordinate System Basics

The coordinate plane is formed by two perpendicular number lines (axes).

x-axis: horizontal (left-right) y-axis: vertical (up-down) Origin: (0, 0) where axes meet Ordered pair: (x, y) ‚Üí x first, y second

Four Quadrants

Quadrant I: (+, +)
x positive, y positive
Quadrant II: (-, +)
x negative, y positive
Quadrant III: (-, -)
x negative, y negative
Quadrant IV: (+, -)
x positive, y negative
