Adding & Subtracting Equations

When you operate on an equation, you must do the same thing to both sides to keep the balance.

‚öñÔ∏è The Golden Rule of Equations

An equation is like a perfectly balanced scale. Whatever you do to the left side, you MUST do the same to the right side. This keeps the equation true.

If x + 3 = 7, and you subtract 3 from the left, you subtract 3 from the right too: x = 7 ‚àí 3 = 4

Systems of Equations

A system of equations is two or more equations with the same variables. Solving means finding values that satisfy ALL equations at once.

ü§î What Does "Solving a System" Mean?

Imagine two linear equations each describing a line on a graph. The solution is the point where the two lines intersect ‚Äî the one (x, y) pair that makes BOTH equations true simultaneously.

There can be: one solution (lines cross), no solution (parallel lines), or infinite solutions (same line).

Glossary of Terms

Quick definitions for every key concept in this course.

Variable
A symbol (usually a letter like x or y) that represents an unknown number you're trying to find.
x + 5 = 12 ‚Üí x is the variable
Coefficient
The number multiplied by a variable in a term. It tells you how many of that variable you have.
In 7x, the coefficient is 7
Constant
A fixed number in an expression ‚Äî it has no variable attached to it and never changes.
In 3x + 5, the constant is 5
Like Terms
Terms that have exactly the same variable(s) with exactly the same exponent(s). Only their coefficients may differ.
4x¬≤ and -7x¬≤ are like terms
Expression
A mathematical phrase with numbers, variables, and operations ‚Äî but NO equals sign. Cannot be "solved."
3x¬≤ + 2x - 1
Equation
A mathematical statement with an equals sign, asserting two expressions are equal. Can be solved.
3x + 2 = 14
System of Equations
Two or more equations with the same variables. Solving means finding values that satisfy ALL equations simultaneously.
y = x+1 and x+y = 5
Exponent
A small number written above and to the right of a base, indicating how many times to multiply the base by itself.
x¬≥ = x¬∑x¬∑x
Base
In an expression with an exponent, the base is the number or variable being raised to a power.
In 5‚Å¥, the base is 5
Polynomial
An expression with one or more terms, each being a variable raised to a non-negative integer exponent.
4x¬≥ - 2x¬≤ + x - 7
Binomial
A polynomial with exactly two terms.
(x + 3) or (2y - 5)
Substitution
A method of solving systems where you express one variable in terms of another and plug it into the other equation.
y=2x ‚Üí plug into x+y=9
Elimination
A method of solving systems by adding or subtracting equations to eliminate one variable.
Add +2y and -2y to cancel y
Distributive Property
A rule stating that a(b+c) = ab + ac. You multiply the outside factor by each term inside the parentheses.
3(x+4) = 3x + 12
FOIL
First, Outer, Inner, Last ‚Äî a method for multiplying two binomials by ensuring every term of one multiplies every term of the other.

Solution
The value(s) of the variable(s) that make an equation (or all equations in a system) true.
x=3, y=7 is a solution
